US Tax Deadlines for Foreign Founders 2026

November 6, 2025 Taxes Incozi Team
Back to Blog

Managing a U.S. company from abroad requires proactive year-end planning to meet tax and compliance obligations. This checklist outlines the essential steps for foreign-owned LLCs and C-Corporations to stay in good standing with the IRS and state authorities.

Step 1: Identify Your Business Structure

Your tax filing requirements are determined by your entity type. Confirm your structure by reviewing your formation documents and IRS EIN confirmation letter.

  • Single-Member LLC: A business with one owner. By default, it is a "disregarded entity" for federal income tax, meaning the owner and business are treated as the same for reporting, but specific filings are still required.
  • Multi-Member LLC: A business with two or more owners (members). By default, it is taxed as a partnership.
  • C-Corporation: A separate legal and tax entity from its owners (shareholders).

Unsure of your structure? Consult with a professional to review your registration paperwork.

Step 2: Gather Financial & Ownership Information

Begin compiling the following data in the last quarter of the year to ensure accurate reporting.

Financial Activity

  • Total annual revenue.
  • All business expenses (services, contractors, software, etc.).
  • Owner contributions (personal funds added to the business).
  • Owner distributions (funds transferred from the business to owners).

Ownership Details

  • Names, addresses, and ownership percentages of all members or shareholders.

Organize Records: Use accounting software statements or download annual summaries from your business bank account and payment processors (e.g., Stripe, PayPal). Categorize all transactions.

Note: Multi-member LLCs must allocate profits/losses to each member. C-Corps only report earnings to owners if dividends were paid.

Step 3: Understand Federal Tax Filing Requirements

All U.S. entities must file annual returns. The required form depends on your structure.

Entity Type Primary Tax Form(s) Key Purpose & Notes
Single-Member LLC Form 5472 + Pro Forma 1120 Reports financial transactions between the foreign owner and the LLC.
Multi-Member LLC Form 1065 (Partnership Return) Reports business income/expenses and issues Schedule K-1 to each member. Non-resident members may also need Form 1040-NR.
C-Corporation Form 1120 (Corporate Return) Reports corporate income/expenses. Form 5472 is also required if ≥25% foreign-owned.

Critical Deadlines & Penalties

  • March 15: Form 1065 (Multi-Member LLCs)
  • April 15: Forms 1120 and 5472
  • Extensions: File Form 7004 for a 6-month filing extension. Note: This does not extend any tax payment deadline.
  • Penalty Alert: Failure to file Form 5472 incurs a penalty of $25,000 per year, even for inactive businesses.

Step 4: Fulfill State Compliance Obligations

You must also meet requirements in the state where your company is registered, regardless of U.S. income.

  • Delaware LLCs: Pay a $300 annual franchise tax by June 1.
  • Delaware C-Corps: File an annual report and pay variable franchise tax by March 1.
  • Wyoming LLCs & C-Corps: File an annual report and pay a license tax (minimum $60) by your formation anniversary date.

Consequence of Non-Compliance: Missing state filings can lead to loss of "good standing" or administrative dissolution, jeopardizing your business bank accounts and payment processors.

Step 5: Reconcile Banking & Payment Platforms

The IRS scrutinizes transactions between foreign-owned businesses and their owners. Reconcile your financial data to ensure accuracy.

  1. Download annual transaction reports from all platforms used (e.g., Stripe, PayPal, Mercury, Brex).
  2. Compare these reports with your business bank statements to:
    • Confirm total income.
    • Verify all business expenses.
    • Document any owner draws (LLCs) or dividends (C-Corps).

Clean, reconciled data is essential for accurate tax preparation and audit defense.

Step 6: Partner with a Professional Service

For remote, hassle-free compliance, consider a specialized service. A typical professional engagement includes:

  1. Initial Consultation: A free review of your business structure and history.
  2. Onboarding: Signing an engagement letter and providing financial/ownership details via a secure portal.
  3. Preparation & Review: The professional team prepares all necessary tax forms.
  4. Filing & Support: Forms are submitted to the IRS, and the team provides ongoing support for any notices.

This approach eliminates the need to navigate complex U.S. tax codes personally.

Final Summary

Staying compliant involves three core actions:

  1. Confirm your business structure.
  2. Organize your financial and ownership data.
  3. File the correct federal and state returns by their deadlines.

By planning ahead and leveraging professional expertise, you can effectively manage your U.S. entity's obligations from anywhere in the world.
